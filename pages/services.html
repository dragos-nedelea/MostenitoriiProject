Ceremony Dances

"Podul de flori"

The tradition of the bridge of flowers at weddings is a popular practice in many cultures and countries around the world. This involves creating a decorative bridge, made of flowers or other decorative materials, that the bride and groom cross as they make their way to the altar or during the wedding party. The bridge of flowers symbolizes the transition from the life of a bachelor or from the life of a girl to the life of a married couple. As they walk through the bridge of flowers, the bride and groom are encouraged to focus on their love and commitment to each other as they symbolize an important transition in their lives. This tradition can be customized according to the tastes and preferences of each couple with different types of flowers, colors and decoration styles. The flower bridge can be placed in different locations - outside the church or inside the wedding hall - and can be created with the help of family or friends, who can contribute flowers or other decorative materials.

"Încuscritul"

The tradition of incarceration is one of the oldest and most important traditions of Romanian weddings and involves a man from the groom's family going to the bride's house to propose to her. This ceremony takes place before the wedding and is considered a symbol of respect, tradition and family unity. In the enshrinement ceremony, the man who proposes to the bride brings with him gifts for her family, such as bread and salt, symbols of prosperity and well-being. Before being allowed into the house, the man must go through several preparatory tests, which may include dancing with the bride or even negotiating a price to take the bride. If the man manages to successfully complete all these tests, then the bride is given in marriage and the two will unite their destinies in a festive way at the wedding. In this way, the tradition of the enshrined is a particularly important moment for both families and is considered a way to strengthen the bonds between them.

"Colacul Miresei"

The bride's veil is a tradition at Romanian weddings, which represents a symbol of unity and the bond between the bride and groom. The cola is usually a ring made of bread, decorated with flowers, ribbons and other decorative elements. During the wedding ceremony, the bride and groom take the coil in their hands and break it together, thus symbolizing that they will be united and share everything in their life together.After the coil is broken, each of the two will take a piece and put it in the other's mouth, thus symbolizing the promise to feed and support each other for the rest of their lives. In some regions of the country, the bride's cake is made of a sweet dough, decorated with raisins or other dried fruits, and in other regions it is made of a simpler dough, without added sugar. The tradition of the bride's bun is an old practice that dates back to ancient times and is still respected and practiced in many areas of Romania. This tradition is considered an important moment of the wedding ceremony and a symbol of love and unity between the bride and groom.
