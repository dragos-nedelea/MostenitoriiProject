About Us

The folk dances, together with the songs, legends, folk poetry, traditions are our national treasure -the treasure of folk pearls inherited from our ancestors. It is considered, and we agree with this opinion, that the dance is the hidden language of our soul, or, in other words, the visualization of our ardor and the most sublime spiritual impulses. ...What does it mean to dance? It would seem that it is a simple question but each one has his own answer. Why? Perhaps also because, for us, the choreographers, to dance is synonymous with to breathe. In fact, most people have the necessity to manifest themselves in and through dance and the intuition tells them that they could achieve success since early childhood. I mean spiritual satisfaction... Dance is my passion and love but it also enters my professional duties - to teach my little and clumsy pupils (brought at Mostenitorii at the beginning by hand by parents, brothers, sisters or grandparents) to move in accordance with the rhythm, to learn the steps of bora or any other dance. It is an enormous joy to see them later dancing professionally and to bathe in the applauses with which only artists are rewarded. I am very glad that my work is not in vain, I would rather say it brings great satisfaction, because there is not a greater appreciation than the one that is reflected in the eyes of the parents and their children. Dance, if to say in appropriate terms, is the lust of the soul and it is the joy of the body. The one who is dancing bora is satisfying a spiritual need - otherwise why would people dance? We, the Moldovans, use every opportunity to manifest ourselves in dance: horas, weddings, baptism, and any party. By means of dance we express our feelings-the joy or the sorrow, love, thirst for life, enthusiasm... Perhaps this explains the existence of so many choreographic ensembles, the multitude of folk dances inherited from the ancestors that we are obliged to preserve for present and future generations. I think I am right saying that only preserving and valorizing our folklore we would preserve ourselves as a distinct nation. The members of the ensemble are profoundly in love with our folk dance, genuine national costume, our nation's songs and music. The ensemble of folk dances Mostenitorii, was founded in 1994 and it had a small team- only 16 children and teenagers. In three years, in 1997, having already 48 members the team became winner of the national festival for children Alunelul The declared - but also proven! - mission of the ensemble is to valorize the traditional culture, the national dress, the costumes, the folk traditions and games that are characteristic for the region we are representing. The scions of the strong tree called Mostenitorii contributed and are fully contributing through dedication, talent and love towards dance to the preservation of nation's inheritance. In a relatively short period of time the team advanced rapidly, becoming one of the best ensembles of such a profile (children) in the country and, respectively, enjoying the appreciation of the public. The list of successes comprise: the honorific title model artistic ensemble, laureate at several national and international contests and festivals. During two decades of activity (initially, Circle of folk dances from the Center of Creation for Children from Riscani town), 400 children were taught in the ensemble Mostenitorii. At present the ensemble is attended by 140 children of 6-19 years old, divided, in accordance to the age, into three choreographic groups: preparatory, medium and high. The team presented hundreds of shows on the most important stages in country and abroad and appeared for numerous times on local and international TV. The actual repertoire of the ensemble consists of folk dances which reflect the most famous traditions, customs and habits: the wedding dance Zestrea, Capra, Caiutii, the procession of carol singers, the famous Ball of the conductors etc. They delight the eyes of the spectators in the concert programs and get the deserved applauses as the dance is a good opportunity for the assertion of national identity. The beauty of the dance is completed and better valorized by the national costume. Each member of the ensemble, former or actual, has his own costume, which, obviously, is a genuine one. The ensemble of folk dances Mostenitorii from Riscani town through everything it is doing, contributes to the preservation and valorization of the cultural richness and traditional choreographic values. The talent, dedication and perseverance are the elements that define our team.

Repertory

"Coșerul și Hangul"

Traditional dance in which they caught each other the men, who really had a coordination of muscles, arms, legs, head, very good, very developed. The men who knew this dance very well were caught.

"Ciuleandra"

Old folk dance, with origins in Muntenia Subcarpathia, is considered a Dionysian dance, in which each of the participants is slowly attracted by the frenzy of the rhythm.

"Polca din bătrâni"

A characteristic dance tread back and forth, The very cries this dance it sounds like this: "And-forward and-back, that's how the polka is with us, And to right and to left, look, that's how pride plays!".

"Suita de dansuri românești"

In his own way he dances calmly, with steps back and forth, danced in pairs, at the end of which the tempo se modifies, the so-called "Floricica - game of two".

"Dans țigănesc"

During his time the "Gypsy" dances were taken over from the Bulgarians, Russians, Armenians and gypsies. Borrowing the separate elements and rhythms from the dances other peoples, the Moldovans interpret them in their own way - with a lot of fire, temperament and in a way very special. None of the aforementioned peoples have dances similar.

"Joc Mare"

Game that gathers in a big circle the whole assembly. The dancers hold hands, walking diagonally separating into 2 lines, followed by game in pairs then going backwards, going to the left the dancers bow the public, then the movement is repeated until a semicircle is formed.

"Hora de Sărbătoare"

It is a peasant dance that brings people together in a large circle the whole assembly. The dancers holding hands, taking 3 diagonal steps forward, L-R-L, followed by o fight with right, then back, three steps R-L-R, a beat with the left, at the same time turning the circle, basically counterclockwise.

"Arcanul"

The Arcanus dance was performed in chorus open, taking a step forward with the foot left, moved behind, secretly, with the same steps, continue the game comprising in his choreography the snake walk and its symbol. The form to express the sacrifice, and we can interpret it in several ways. The three converging lines can represent a spear, a root or a man. The spear is the god's weapon, and in his pose by patron of war and victory, he promised those who wore this sign win and protection.
